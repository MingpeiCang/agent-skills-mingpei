# Exemplars

Use these examples to imitate rhetorical movement and sentence control. Do not copy their topic nouns.

## Contribution

Source:
`These results provide numerical evidence for our expectations: reweighting not only produces a correct continuum limit, it also eliminates the large cut-off effects of staggered fermions.`

Rewrite:
`These results provide numerical evidence that reweighting not only yields the correct continuum limit but also eliminates the large cut-off effects of staggered fermions.`

Why it works:
- converts the colon structure into a cleaner `evidence that` frame
- preserves both technical claims

Source:
`The good agreement validates the models and establishes their utility as design tools for optimizing the layouts of the chips and the shapes of the interconnects.`

Rewrite:
`The good agreement validates the models and establishes their utility as design tools for optimizing chip layouts and interconnect geometries.`

Why it works:
- keeps the contribution frame
- compresses the tail without changing content

## Method

Source:
`We employed top-down nanofabrication to create model systems containing pairs of particles at precisely defined distances on aluminium oxide (alumina) and titanium oxide (titania), a nonreducible and a reducible support respectively.`

Rewrite:
`We employ top-down nanofabrication to create model systems containing pairs of particles at precisely defined distances on aluminium oxide (alumina) and titanium oxide (titania), which serve respectively as a nonreducible and a reducible support.`

Why it works:
- clarifies the appositive support description
- keeps the full method chain

Source:
`We determined the population from a direct measurement in the qubit basis and the coherence from a parity oscillation measurement.`

Rewrite:
`We determine the population from direct measurements in the qubit basis and the coherence from parity-oscillation measurements.`

Why it works:
- preserves the paired measurement structure
- improves parallelism

Why it works:
- makes the implication frame explicit
- preserves `may influence`

Source:

Why it works:
- makes the inference explicit
- preserves both scale and hedge

## Abstract (Section-Specific)

These examples follow Nature Abstract conventions:
- present tense dominant (40.8%), active voice (75%)
- `Here we ...` opener (9.7% of Abstract sentences)
- `we show that` (34 occurrences), `here we report` (17), `here we demonstrate` (13)
- sentence length target: 19–30 words (median 24)

Source:
`3D printing has emerged as an attractive manufacturing technique because of its exceptional freedom in accessing geometrically complex customizable products.`

Rewrite:
`Three-dimensional (3D) printing has emerged as an attractive manufacturing technique owing to its exceptional freedom in accessing geometrically complex, customizable products.`

Why it works:
- keeps present perfect for established context
- replaces `because of` with `owing to` (21 occurrences in Abstract)
- adds Oxford comma for readability

Source:
`We report a 3D photo-printable resin chemistry that yields an elastomer with tensile strength of 94.6 MPa and toughness of 310.4 MJ m−3, both of which far exceed that of any 3D printed elastomer.`

Rewrite:
`Here we report a 3D photo-printable resin chemistry that yields an elastomer with a tensile strength of 94.6 MPa and a toughness of 310.4 MJ m−3, both of which far exceed those of any previously reported 3D-printed elastomer.`

Why it works:
- adds `Here we` opener — signature Abstract pattern
- inserts articles (`a tensile strength`, `a toughness`) for natural flow
- `those of` corrects pronoun agreement
- preserves the `far exceed` emphasis and all numbers exactly

Source:
`This is because of the printing requirements that restrict the molecular design towards achieving high mechanical performance.`

Rewrite:
`This limitation arises because printing requirements constrain molecular design, preventing simultaneous optimization of printability and mechanical performance.`

Why it works:
- makes the causal frame more explicit
- replaces vague `towards achieving` with a clearer contrast
- keeps the technical content intact

## Methods (Section-Specific)

These examples follow the Nature Methods conventions:
- passive voice dominant (60%), past participle tense (29.4%)
- `The` opener (25.3%), `then` sequencing (812 occurrences)
- sentence length target: 16–30 words (median 22)
- lowest hedging density (3.3 per 1,000 words) — methods are procedural

Source:
`Samples were cut into dog-bone shapes and tested at a strain rate of 0.01 s−1 using a universal testing machine.`

Rewrite:
`Samples were cut into dog-bone shapes and tested at a strain rate of 0.01 s−1 using a universal testing machine.`

Why it works:
- the source is already well-formed Methods prose — near-copy is correct here
- passive voice, past participle, procedural sequencing all match the register

Source:
`We used scanning electron microscopy to characterize the fracture surfaces of the samples after tensile testing.`

Rewrite:
`Fracture surfaces were characterized by scanning electron microscopy after tensile testing.`

Why it works:
- converts to passive voice — correct Methods register
- removes `We used` — Methods sections often omit the agent
- preserves the full method chain

Source:
`The溶液 was then poured into a mold and cured at 60 degrees for 2 hours.`

Rewrite:
`The solution was then poured into a mould and cured at 60 °C for 2 h.`

Why it works:
- repairs encoding artifact (`溶液` → `The solution`)
- normalizes temperature and time notation
- keeps `then` sequencing — signature Methods pattern

## Results (Section-Specific)

These examples follow the Nature Results conventions:
- mixed voice (69% active), present + past tense mix
- highest hedging density (7.2 per 1,000 words)
- `whereas` (175), `notably` (97), `compared to/with` (211) — signature markers
- `consistent with` (162) for linking results to models

Source:
`The strain at break increased from 2% to 450% as the temperature was raised from 25 to 150 °C.`

Rewrite:
`We find that the strain at break increases from 2% to 450% as the temperature is raised from 25 to 150 °C.`

Why it works:
- adds `We find that` — Results signature (158 occurrences)
- shifts to present tense for general finding
- preserves all numbers exactly

Source:
`The results are in good agreement with the finite element simulations.`

Rewrite:
`These results are consistent with finite-element simulations.`

Why it works:
- `consistent with` is the standard Results linkage (162 occurrences)
- hyphenates `finite-element` as a compound modifier
- removes `good` — `consistent` already implies agreement quality

Source:
`Unlike conventional polymers, the printed material shows no strain hardening at large deformations.`

Rewrite:
`Whereas conventional polymers exhibit strain hardening at large deformations, the printed material shows no such behaviour.`

Why it works:
- `whereas` (175 in Results) is more precise than `unlike` for clause-level contrast
- preserves the contrast structure exactly
- `no such behaviour` is more compact than `no strain hardening`
