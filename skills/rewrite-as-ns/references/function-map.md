# Function Map

Classify the source before rewriting. The function determines how much restructuring is helpful.

## `contribution_or_implication`

Typical cues:

- `here we demonstrate`
- `this study provides`
- `our work ...`
- `these results provide evidence`
- `enables`, `establishes`, `opens`

Preferred move:

- make the contribution frame explicit
- preserve both claims if the sentence makes two linked contributions
- prefer `evidence that`, `demonstrate that`, `establish`, `enable`

Stay close when:

- the sentence is already author-framed and well formed

## `method_statement`

Typical cues:

- `we use`
- `we employ`
- `we developed`
- `we constructed`
- `we measured`
- `here we use`

Preferred move:

- keep the method -> purpose -> target chain explicit
- fix list parallelism and awkward appositives
- often only a small rewrite is needed

Stay close when:

- the sentence is already concise and structurally sound

## `result_statement`

Typical cues:

- observed trend
- quantitative relation
- behavior under conditions
- unresolved or limiting result

Preferred move:

- surface the result with `we find that`, `we show that`, `we observe that` when it improves clarity
- make monotonic and causal relations explicit
- preserve limitations and exceptions

Stay close when:

- the sentence is already a clean result statement

## `scope_or_implication`

Typical cues:

- `may`, `could`, `can`
- `suggests that`
- `would benefit`
- `in principle`
- applicability or design implication

Preferred move:

- preserve the hedge
- make the implication explicit only if the source already supports it
- keep scale, condition, and applicability boundaries intact

Stay close when:

- the sentence already expresses scope with the right hedge strength

## `logic_transition`

Typical cues:

- `however`
- `by contrast`
- `in contrast`
- `therefore`
- `as a result`
- `furthermore`

Preferred move:

- preserve the discourse relation first
- then improve local syntax, agreement, or compactness
- transitions often benefit from minimal edits rather than major paraphrase

Stay close when:

- the opener and logical contrast are already clear
