# Lexicon Bank

This file expands the usable style lexicon from the distilled outputs. Use it to increase variation without drifting away from the target voice.

## How To Use This File

- Prefer the `style_core` items first.
- Use the `secondary academic` items to avoid repetition, not to define the voice on their own.
- Match the lexical choice to sentence function.
- Do not insert a preferred word if a plain source word is more accurate.

## Primary Style-Core Verbs

These are the main voice markers.

- contribution / implication: `provide`, `enable`, `suggest`, `support`, `reveal`, `establish`, `capture`
- result-compatible when justified: `reveal`, `indicate`, `confirm`

## Secondary Academic Verbs

These occur frequently in the corpus and are safe for rotation when the function matches, but they are more generic and should not dominate the style.

- result: `show`, `find`, `observe`, `measure`, `obtain`, `identify`
- method: `use`, `employ`, `design`, `develop`, `construct`, `determine`, `quantify`, `optimize`, `integrate`
- transition / inference: `attribute`, `indicate`, `report`, `compare`, `validate`

## Adjectives

Primary style-core:

- `important`, `wide`, `general`, `robust`, `relevant`, `quantitative`, `mechanistic`, `novel`, `applicable`

Secondary but safe in moderation:

- `high`, `low`, `large`, `small`, `strong`, `effective`, `consistent`, `broad`, `direct`, `practical`

Use the secondary set only when the source already implies that evaluation.

## Adverbs

Primary style-core:

- `directly`, `generally`, `readily`, `widely`, `closely`, `precisely`, `broadly`

Secondary but safe in moderation:

- `therefore`, `thus`, `however`, `previously`, `typically`, `fully`, `approximately`, `also`

These are mostly discourse or generic academic adverbs; do not let them crowd out the stronger style-core items.

## Function-Scoped Lexical Rotation

Use these rotations when many nearby sentences serve the same function.

### Contribution / implication

- `provide evidence that ...`
- `provide insight into ...`
- `reveal an important role for ...`
- `establish quantitative agreement between ...`
- `capture the key features of ...`
- `enable ...`
- `support a mechanistic picture of ...`

### Result

- `we show that ...`
- `we find that ...`
- `we observe that ...`
- `our results indicate that ...`
- `these results demonstrate ...`
- `we measure ... directly`
- `we obtain ... consistent with ...`
- `we identify a transition from ... to ...`

### Method

- `we use ... to ...`
- `we employ ... to ...`
- `we develop ... for ...`
- `we design ... to enable ...`
- `we determine ... by ...`
- `we quantify ... from ...`
- `we construct ... by integrating ...`
- `we optimize ... by varying ...`

### Scope / implication

- `this suggests that ...`
- `these findings may enable ...`
- `these findings may inform ...`
- `these findings may guide ...`
- `this framework provides a basis for ...`
- `the method can be generalized to ...`
- `this approach may be useful for ...`
- `this observation may help reconcile ...`

### Transition

- `however, ...`
- `by contrast, ...`
- `in contrast, ...`
- `therefore, ...`
- `as a result, ...`
- `in addition, ...`
- `furthermore, ...`
- `moreover, ...`
- `taken together, ...`
- `in summary, ...`

## Openers Worth Reusing

These appear frequently enough to support the style, but they should be rotated rather than repeated mechanically.

- contribution: `here we`, `in this work`, `our results`, `these findings`
- result: `we find`, `we show`, `we observe`, `these results`, `our results`
- method: `we use`, `we employ`, `we developed`, `we constructed`, `we determined`
- implication: `this suggests`, `in principle`, `this approach`, `the framework`
- transition: `however`, `by contrast`, `as a result`, `in addition`, `furthermore`, `moreover`, `taken together`, `in summary`

## Red Flags

- Avoid using `foundation for ...` as a general contribution verb unless the source truly makes a downstream-enablement claim.
- Avoid using too many `novel`, `important`, or `robust` evaluations in a row.
- Avoid stacking multiple style-core items in one sentence just because they are on-brand.
