# Template Bank

These are structural patterns distilled from the curated syntax library. Use them as a controlled source of variation. They are not fill-in-the-blank commands.

## Use Rules

- Prefer the low-risk frames first.
- Escalate to medium-risk frames only when the source clearly supports the stronger rhetorical move.
- Treat high-risk frames as rare options for source sentences that already make that exact kind of claim.

## Contribution / implication

Low-risk:

- `We provide evidence that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].`
- `These findings provide insight into [PROCESS].`
- `Our results reveal an important role for [SYSTEM] in [PROCESS].`
- `We identify a [SYSTEM] that captures the key features of [PROCESS].`
- `This framework allows direct comparison between [SYSTEM] and [SYSTEM].`
- `This strategy provides a practical route toward [GOAL].`
- `Together, these results suggest a general strategy for [GOAL].`

Medium-risk:

- `Here we report a [SYSTEM] that enables [CLAIM].`
- `Here we demonstrate a [METHOD] for [PROCESS].`
- `In this work, we establish [CLAIM] by [METHOD].`
- `Our results establish quantitative agreement between [MODEL] and [MEASUREMENT].`
- `These findings support a mechanistic picture of [PROCESS].`

High-risk:

- `Our work lays the foundation for [APPLICATION].`
- `We provide a general strategy for [GOAL].`

Use high-risk frames only when the source already makes an enabling or broad generalization claim.

### Abstract Contribution Templates

These are the signature Nature Abstract patterns:

- `Here we report a [SYSTEM] that [VERB] [PROPERTY] of [VALUE].` (17 occurrences)
- `Here we show that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (23 occurrences)
- `Here we demonstrate [METHOD/CLAIM].` (13 occurrences)
- `Here we present [SYSTEM/METHOD] that [VERB].` (10 occurrences)
- `We show that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY], [which/both of which] [RESULT].` (34 occurrences)
- `Our work suggests [IMPLICATION].`
- `This [METHOD] yields [PROPERTY] of [VALUE], both of which far exceed [BASELINE].`

Use `Here we ...` only in the Abstract or when the source explicitly uses an author-framed contribution sentence. Do not inject it into Results or Methods.

## Result

Low-risk:

- `We find that [PROPERTY] is directly related to [PROPERTY].`
- `Our results indicate that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].`
- `These results demonstrate a strong dependence on [CONDITION].`
- `We measure [PROPERTY] directly from [SYSTEM].`
- `We obtain [PROPERTY] consistent with [MODEL].`
- `We identify a transition from [STATE_A] to [STATE_B].`
- `We find quantitative agreement between [MODEL] and [MEASUREMENT].`

Medium-risk:

- `We show that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY] under [CONDITION].`
- `We observe that [SYSTEM] undergoes a clear change in [PROPERTY].`
- `Our results reveal a substantial enhancement in [PROPERTY].`
- `These observations reveal an unexpected role for [SYSTEM].`
- `We report a marked improvement in [PROPERTY] after [METHOD].`

High-risk:

- `In contrast, we observe the opposite effect: [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].`

Use the high-risk frame only when the source genuinely contains a contrastive result.

### Results-Specific Templates

Results sections use the highest hedging density (7.2 per 1,000 words) and the most contrast structures.

**Result presentation** (active author frame):
- `We find that [PROPERTY] [increases/decreases] with [VARIABLE].` (158 occurrences of `find` in Results)
- `We observe [PHENOMENON] in [SYSTEM].` (171 occurrences of `observe`)
- `We show that [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (482 occurrences of `show`)
- `Our results indicate that [CLAIM].` (145 occurrences of `indicate`)

**Hedged result presentation:**
- `These results suggest that [SYSTEM] may [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (163 occurrences of `suggest`)
- `This observation is consistent with [MODEL/PREDICTION].` (162 occurrences of `consistent`)
- `We attribute [PHENOMENOM] to [MECHANISM].`

**Contrast structures** (Results has the highest contrast density):
- `Whereas [SYSTEM_A] [VERB], [SYSTEM_B] [VERB].` (175 occurrences of `whereas` in Results)
- `Unlike [SYSTEM_A], [SYSTEM_B] [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (23 occurrences)
- `By contrast, [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (80 occurrences)
- `In contrast, [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY].` (37 occurrences)

**Quantitative comparison:**
- `Compared to [BASELINE], [SYSTEM] shows [PROPERTY] of [VALUE].` (211 occurrences in Results)
- `[PROPERTY] increases by [FACTOR] compared with [BASELINE].`
- `[SYSTEM] exceeds [BASELINE] by [FACTOR/ORDERS_OF_MAGNITUDE].`

**Emphasis** (use sparingly):
- `Notably, [OBSERVATION].` (97 occurrences — Results signature)
- `[SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY] by orders of magnitude.` (20 occurrences)
- `Indeed, [CONFIRMATION].` (67 occurrences in Results)

## Method

Low-risk:

- `We use [METHOD] to quantify [PROPERTY] in [SYSTEM].`
- `We employ [METHOD] to probe [PROCESS] in [SYSTEM].`
- `We employ [METHOD] to identify the origin of [PROPERTY].`
- `We measure [PROPERTY] using [METHOD] and compare the result with [MODEL].`
- `We determine [PROPERTY] by fitting [MODEL] to [MEASUREMENT].`
- `We quantify [PROPERTY] from [MEASUREMENT] under [CONDITION].`
- `We construct [SYSTEM] by integrating [SYSTEM] with [SYSTEM].`
- `We optimize [SYSTEM] by varying [CONDITION] and monitoring [PROPERTY].`

Medium-risk:

- `We design [SYSTEM] to enable direct [MEASUREMENT] of [PROPERTY].`
- `We develop [METHOD] for [PROCESS].`
- `We develop [SYSTEM] that allows precise control over [PROPERTY].`
- `We develop [METHOD] that combines [METHOD] with [METHOD].`
- `We design [METHOD] that captures the essential features of [PROCESS].`

High-risk:

- `We develop [SYSTEM] for large-scale [APPLICATION].`
- `We design [SYSTEM] for high performance under [CONDITION].`

Use high-risk frames only when the source explicitly claims scale or performance design.

### Methods-Specific Templates

Methods sections are 60% passive. Use these patterns when rewriting Methods text:

**Passive constructions** (dominant in Methods):
- `[SYSTEM] was/were [VERB-ed] using [METHOD].`
- `[PROPERTY] was determined by [METHOD].`
- `[SYSTEM] was prepared by [PROCESS].`
- `Samples were [VERB-ed] at [CONDITION] for [DURATION].`
- `[PROPERTY] was measured using [INSTRUMENT].`
- `[VALUE] was obtained from [MEASUREMENT].`

**Active constructions** (less common but acceptable in Methods):
- `We use [METHOD] to [VERB] [PROPERTY].`
- `We performed [ANALYSIS] on [SYSTEM].`
- `We determined [PROPERTY] by [METHOD].`

**Procedural sequencing** (`then` is the #1 Methods adverb, 812 occurrences):
- `Samples were first [VERB-ed], then [VERB-ed] at [CONDITION].`
- `After [STEP], [SYSTEM] was [VERB-ed] using [METHOD].`
- `[SYSTEM] was [VERB-ed] at [CONDITION] for [DURATION], followed by [STEP].`

**Measurement and quantification:**
- `[PROPERTY] was quantified from [MEASUREMENT] under [CONDITION].`
- `[PROPERTY] was calculated using [EQUATION/MODEL].`
- `The [PROPERTY] of [SYSTEM] was measured at [CONDITION].`

**Pairwise comparison** (`respectively` is #6 Methods adverb, 311 occurrences):
- `[SYSTEM_A] and [SYSTEM_B] had [PROPERTY] of [VALUE_A] and [VALUE_B], respectively.`
- `Non-reducible and reducible supports served as [SYSTEM_A] and [SYSTEM_B], respectively.`

## Scope / implication

Low-risk:

- `This suggests that [SYSTEM] may [VERB] [PROPERTY].`
- `These findings may inform the design of [SYSTEM].`
- `These findings may guide future studies of [PROCESS].`
- `This framework provides a basis for understanding [PROCESS].`
- `The framework can be adapted for [APPLICATION].`
- `The same logic can be used to interpret [PROPERTY] in [SYSTEM].`
- `This observation may help reconcile [MODEL] with [MEASUREMENT].`

Medium-risk:

- `This suggests a practical strategy for [APPLICATION].`
- `These findings may enable [APPLICATION] across [CONDITION].`
- `The method can be generalized to [SYSTEM].`
- `This approach may be useful for [APPLICATION].`
- `This strategy may enable precise control over [PROPERTY].`
- `This result may open a path toward [GOAL].`

High-risk:

- `This approach should be applicable to [SYSTEM].`
- `This strategy could be extended to [SYSTEM] with minimal modification.`

Use high-risk frames only when the source already implies strong transferability.

## Logic transition

Low-risk:

- `However, [PROPERTY] is limited by [CONDITION].`
- `However, this interpretation does not explain [PROPERTY].`
- `By contrast, [SYSTEM] remains [ADJECTIVE] across [CONDITION].`
- `As a result, [SYSTEM] exhibits [PROPERTY].`
- `In addition, [SYSTEM] enables direct [MEASUREMENT] of [PROPERTY].`
- `Taken together, these observations support [CLAIM].`
- `Taken together, these results reveal a consistent pattern across [SYSTEM].`

Medium-risk:

- `Therefore, the observed [PROPERTY] can be attributed to [PROCESS].`
- `Therefore, [SYSTEM] offers a direct way to test [CLAIM].`
- `On the basis of these observations, we propose [CLAIM].`
- `On the basis of these results, we identify [PROCESS] as the dominant factor.`
- `Furthermore, this design improves [PROPERTY] without sacrificing [PROPERTY].`
- `Moreover, the same framework explains [PROPERTY] in [SYSTEM].`
- `In summary, we demonstrate [CLAIM] with [SYSTEM].`

High-risk:

- `In contrast, [SYSTEM] [VERB] [PROPERTY] rather than [PROPERTY].`

Use the high-risk frame only when the sentence truly contrasts two outcomes within one clause.
